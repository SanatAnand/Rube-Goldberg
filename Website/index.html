Behind The Scene

Let's have a look at what's behind the machinery and what it takes to make the machine

Ideation

It takes a lot of time and effort to think about the sequence of events. According to Wikipedia, A Rube Goldberg machine is a contraption, invention, device or apparatus that is deliberately over-engineered to perform a simple task in a complicated fashion, usually including a chain reaction. Therefore the entire sequence needs to be figured out and the reliability has to be realised.

C++

Decent Knowledge and Practice in C++ is required that is the main framework for this project. The enitre project is coded in C++. It requires knowledge of Object Oriented Programming (OOP's), Virtual functions and other advanced C++ features.

Box 2D

Box2D is an open source C++ engine for simulating rigid bodies in 2D, developed by Erin Catto. Box2D performs constrained rigid body simulation. It can simulate bodies composed of convex polygons, circles, and edge shapes. Bodies are joined together with joints and acted upon by forces. The engine also applies gravity, friction, and restitution.

Components of the Rube-Goldberg Machine

Let's have a look at the various components of the machine and the role they play in the entire simulation. They have been made using various types of objects that follow all Laws of Physics.

The Entire Machine

The Entire Rube Golderg Machine

Ball Hinge System

A Series of Steps with a hinge at the end of each stair trigerred by a ball on the previous level triggering another ball on the current level

Dam

The Dam that releases the water that triggers the entire sequence to extinguish the fire

Fire

The Fire that will be extinguished at the end of the entire simulation

Flying Helium Balloon

The Flying Helium Balloon let off by the Air Pump

Air Pump

An Air Pump that lets out a balloon on being pushed down

Gate Mechanism

The Gate that is triggered by intermediate steps that lets go of a ball that in turn triggers the fire extinguisher

Water Collector

The Water Collector that captures the water left by the dam and takes it back to the dam thus saving water

Control Gate

-The Gate that opens with the help of the rotating action of the turbine

Dominos Mechanism

Three Layered Stacked Dominos that transfer the momentum

Loop-A-Loop

A Loop-A-Loop Tunnel for a ball that reverses its motion in a classical swirly way

Turbine

A turbine driven by the water released by the dam

Lift

A Lift that carries the water collector back to the top of the Dam

Heavy Ball

A Heavy Ball that releases the ball in the bucket for extinguishing the fire

Bucket

The bucket that holds the water for extinguishing the fire

Revolving Plank

Revolving Plank that releases the water collector once it fills up with the water released by the dam intially
